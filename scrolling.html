Early LAX

The original Los Angeles International Airport was established in 1928 as Mines Field. It became LAX in the 1940s, initially serving as a modest airfield before evolving into one of the world's busiest airports. The early terminals, roads, and runways reflected the automobile-centric planning of mid-century Los Angeles.
